Just like the ? meant a character is optional, + would mean that the character (or pattern) appears at least once.

A good tracker can handle whatever is thrown at him, so your next challenge isn't about animals, rather finding me phone numbers of two foxy ladies I met last night. They appear here in this paper filled with number but they have a very strict format: they always start with a "0" followed by 1 or 2 digits, space and exactly 3 more digits, another space and then at least 6 digits

You can limit the number of appearances using the `{}` pattern: {x,y} would mean between x and y, {x,} would mean at least x and {x} would mean exactly x

Your next task is to find all the tigers including their counts, but this is tricky because there's the tiger's description we need to ignore. Essentially the description is anything but a number, so you can use the \D to catch anything that is not a digit (and you already know to use the + to catch at least one character

But it's even tricker because you need to make sure tigers' description is as short as possible (try out to see what happens). To make RegExp do a lazy (and not greedy) you'll need to use +?
